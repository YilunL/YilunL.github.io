Hi! My name is Ivan Li, and I am currently an Assistant Professor of Marketing at the Jindal School of Management at University of Texas at Dallas.

I am a marketing researcher who believes that tools from economics and empirical industrial organization are well suited to answer questions in marketing contexts. My research leverages applied theory, structural econometrics, and empirical data from pop culture contexts to study general phenomena in marketing and economics, such as disclosure, intermediation, and bundling. My current research projects revolve around influencer marketing and college football.

While you're here, check out my latest research projects - including the code relating to my research.

If you're interested in connecting with me, my social media pages are in the footers of every page, or on the contact page.

If you're an UPenn undergrad, check out the UPenn tab in the header.

Bio

I am originally from Brookings, South Dakota but now consider the Dallas, Texas area my home after my parents moved here post-highschool graduation. I obtained my Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Science from the University of Pennsylvania and my Ph.D. in Marketing from the Graduate School of Business at Stanford University.

As for my hobbies, I follow the NBA closely; my favorite team is the Los Angeles Lakers. I watch a lot of sports in general - I'm always plugged into what's going on in football, soccer, baseball, etc. I watch a ton of Youtube and Twitch streams ... guess where inspiration for my research comes from? I also enjoy listening to pop, hiphop, R&B, and classical music. I play way too much League of Legends during my free time.
